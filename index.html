Slow Fashion

The slow fashion movement started in 2007 when Kate Fletcher, an English activist,
writer, designer, and sustainability professor coined the concept as a counterpart and response to fast fashion.
She defines slow fashion as quality-based rather than time-based. Other slow fashion pioneers note that the
movement encourages slower production, unifies sustainability with ethics, and ultimately invites consumers to
invest in well-made and lasting clothes.

Designer Emilia Wick put it this way:
"Slow fashion is a return to a personal relationship with fashion. A fashion where trends and seasons don't matter, but where your ethics and aesthetics blend seamlessly and you can escape the stress of constant consumption by focusing on the style you really like."

5 Tips for Applying Slow Fashion to Your Life

  1. Check out secondhand and vintage stores
  2. Remember the golden rule of cost per wear
  3. Give up quantity for quality
  4. Choose fabric wisely
  5. Take care of your things
